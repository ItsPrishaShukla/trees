Mango Tree (Mangifera indica)

The mango tree (Mangifera indica) is a tropical fruit tree that belongs to the Anacardiaceae family. Revered for its delicious and sweet fruit, the mango tree is native to South Asia but is now cultivated in many tropical and subtropical regions around the world. It is a large evergreen species capable of growing up to 30 meters in height and width.

Mangoes are a major source of food in India, the West Indies, and other places. People eat mangoes fresh and in main dishes and desserts. People also use mangoes to make sauces called chutneys. Mangoes are rich in vitamins A, C.

Banyan Tree (Ficus benghalensis)

Banyan, also called Indian banyan or banyan fig, is a tree of the mulberry family native to the Indian subcontinent. The banyan can reach a height up to 30 metres (100 feet) and spreads laterally indefinitely.

The banyan is considered a particularly meaningful tree in India and other parts of the world, with rich historical and spiritual ties. It is associated with the god of death, Yama, and is often planted near crematoriums outside of villages.

Peepal Tree (Ficus religiosa)

The peepal tree is a large, deciduous tree that can grow up to 30 meters tall, with a trunk diameter of around 3 meters. Its heart-shaped leaves have long drip tips and are known for making a rustling sound in the wind, which contributes to its name "chanchala" (meaning vibrant) in several Indian languages.

The tree produces small figs as fruit, which are green when unripe and turn purple as they mature . It is used in every culture on the base of religion.

Black Plum Tree (Syzygium cumini)

The jamun tree is a tall evergreen tree native to tropical Asia. It can grow up to 30 meters tall. The tree's leaves are oblong-shaped and leathery, and its flowers are small and white. The fruit of the jamun tree is an important food source for many animals, including monkeys, squirrels, and birds.

In Ayurveda, its fruits, seeds, bark, and leaves are used as medicine for treating bleeding disorders and other diseases. Nowadays, its seeds become famous as anti-diabetic medicine.

Neem Tree (Azadirachta indica)

Azadirachta indica, commonly known as neem, margosa, nimtree or Indian lilac,[3] is a tree in the mahogany family Meliaceae. It is one of the two species in the genus Azadirachta. It is native to the Indian subcontinent and to parts of Southeast Asia, but is naturalized and grown around the world in tropical and subtropical areas. Its fruits and seeds are the source of neem oil.

Neem has potential health benefits for several aspects of health, including blood sugar management, as well as benefits for your hair, skin, teeth, liver, and kidneys. However, further human studies are necessary. Neem is POSSIBLY SAFE for most adults when taken by mouth for up to 10 weeks, when applied inside the mouth for up to 6 weeks, or when applied to the skin for up to 2 weeks. When neem is taken in large doses or for long periods of time, it is POSSIBLY UNSAFE. It might harm the kidneys and liver.
